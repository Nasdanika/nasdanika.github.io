A standard interpreter executes an artifact exactly as it was written, warts and all. Direct Semantic Execution does not run the artifact; it runs the knowledge extracted from the artifact.

By loading legacy artifacts into a formal metamodel, we validate their constraints, resolve their cross-references, and enrich them with broader systemic context. Only when we have a structurally sound, highly interconnected semantic model do we attach processor objects to execute the logic. This ensures we are executing a validated enterprise truth, not just parsing legacy syntax.
